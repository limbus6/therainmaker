## Phase 10 — Closing

---

# Phase Overview

Phase 10 represents the **final execution of the transaction**.

After Signing, the deal enters the interim period during which the **Conditions Precedent (CPs)** must be satisfied. Once all conditions are met, the parties proceed to **Closing**, where ownership of the company is formally transferred and the purchase price is paid.

This phase converts the negotiated agreement into a **completed transaction**.

Although most of the strategic work has already been done, deals can still fail at this stage if conditions are not satisfied or if unforeseen events occur.



### Narrative & Strategic Context

Closing is the transfer of power. Bank wires are initiated, and escrow accounts are funded. The tension finally breaks when the clearing house confirms the capital transfer. 

The advisor's role concludes with administrative wrap-up and ensuring the success fee invoice is paid out of the closing funds. It ends with a tombstone announcement and a celebratory dinner, instantly followed by the firm hunting for the next Origination.


---

# Core Objectives

1. Ensure all Conditions Precedent are satisfied
2. Coordinate the operational mechanics of the closing
3. Maintain alignment between buyer and seller
4. Prevent last‑minute disruptions
5. Successfully transfer ownership and funds

---

# Key Closing Components

Typical closing activities include:

- confirmation that all Conditions Precedent are satisfied
- regulatory approvals obtained
- financing drawdown by the buyer
- payment of the purchase price
- transfer of shares
- execution of final legal documents

Closing may occur immediately after signing or after a longer interim period depending on regulatory requirements.

---

# Key Variables

CPCompletion (0–100)

Measures how close the process is to satisfying all Conditions Precedent.

RegulatoryApprovalStatus (0–100)

Represents the progress of required regulatory approvals.

FinancingReadiness (0–100)

Measures whether the buyer has secured the necessary funds.

ClosingCertainty (0–100)

Represents the overall probability that the transaction will successfully close.

---

# Phase Workstreams

## Conditions Precedent Tracking

Monitoring the progress of all CPs required before closing.

Typical CPs:

- regulatory approvals
- financing arrangements
- third‑party consents
- internal corporate approvals

---

## Financing Completion

Ensuring that the buyer finalises debt or equity financing required to complete the acquisition.

---

## Legal Closing Preparation

Preparation of closing documentation including:

- share transfer documents
- payment instructions
- board resolutions
- corporate filings

---

## Closing Coordination

Coordinating the final steps between:

- buyer
- seller
- lawyers
- financial advisors
- banks

---

# Player Decisions

Decision: Push for Faster Closing

Pros:
- reduces uncertainty
- accelerates capital realisation

Cons:
- increases operational pressure
- risk of administrative errors

---

Decision: Allow Additional Time for Approvals

Pros:
- increases probability of satisfying CPs

Cons:
- prolongs exposure to deal risk

---

Decision: Manage Public Communication

The advisor may decide whether to:

- announce closing publicly
- maintain confidentiality until completion

---

# Typical Events

Regulatory Approval Granted

Authorities approve the transaction.

---

Regulatory Delay

Approval process takes longer than expected.

---

Financing Delay

Buyer’s financing process slows unexpectedly.

---

Last‑Minute Legal Issue

A technical legal issue emerges before closing.

---

Smooth Closing

All conditions are satisfied ahead of schedule.

---

# Phase Gates

Closing occurs once:

CPCompletion = 100  
FinancingReadiness ≥ 90  
RegulatoryApprovalStatus ≥ required threshold

At that point the purchase price is transferred and the shares change ownership.

---

# Phase Risks

Even at this late stage risks remain:

- failure to obtain regulatory approval
- financing breakdown
- administrative or legal errors
- unexpected market events

These risks may delay or occasionally prevent closing.

---

# Phase Completion

Phase 10 ends when:

OwnershipTransferred = True  
PurchasePricePaid = True

The transaction is now fully completed.

# Design Intent

This phase should feel like **the final execution checkpoint**.

The major strategic decisions are already behind the player, but careful coordination and discipline are required to convert months of work into a completed transaction.

Successful closing represents the culmination of the entire deal process.
