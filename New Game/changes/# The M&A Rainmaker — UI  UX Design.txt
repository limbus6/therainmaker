# The M&A Rainmaker — UI / UX Design System

## 1. Design Philosophy

The interface should simulate the experience of running a live M&A process.

The player is not only managing actions but operating inside a dynamic professional environment where:

* emails arrive
* buyers react
* partners comment
* news appears
* the market moves

The interface should therefore resemble a **professional digital workspace**, inspired by:

Outlook
Slack
DealCloud
Football career mode dashboards

The goal is to create a feeling that the player is **inside an investment banking workflow**.

---

# 2. Core Interface Structure

The main interface is divided into five persistent zones.

Top Navigation Bar
Deal Status Panel
Main Workspace
Communication Center
News & Market Feed

---

# 3. Top Navigation Bar

The top navigation bar represents the control layer of the game.

Displayed elements:

Current Phase
Week / Timeline
Deal Momentum
Risk Level
Team Morale
Budget Remaining
Team Capacity

Example:

Phase: Preparation
Week 3
Momentum: Medium
Risk: Low
Budget: 38
Team Capacity: 12

---

# 4. Deal Status Panel

A persistent panel showing the key metrics of the process.

Displayed variables:

Client Trust
Buyer Interest
Valuation Potential
Preparation Progress
Risk
Team Morale
Deal Momentum

These variables are affected by actions and events.

---

# 5. Main Workspace

The central area where the player performs actions.

It shows:

Available Actions
Action Categories
Current Tasks
Cascade Tasks

Each action card shows:

Action Name
Cost
Work
Complexity
Effect Summary

Example card:

Draft Information Memorandum

Cost: 12
Work: 30
Complexity: High

Effects:

Buyer Interest +10
Preparation Progress +15

---

# 6. Communication Center (Email System)

The communication center is one of the most important narrative tools.

The interface should resemble an **Outlook-like inbox**.

Messages arrive from:

Clients
Partners
Buyers
Team Members
Advisors

The player can open emails and choose responses.

Example email:

Subject: Quick question on the financial model

Sender: CFO

Message:

"We noticed the growth assumptions in the model are higher than expected.
Can you walk us through the logic?"

Player responses:

Explain assumptions
Revise model
Escalate to partner

Each response triggers different outcomes.

---

# 7. Decision Emails

Some emails require immediate decisions.

Examples:

Buyer requests early access
Client pushes for higher valuation
Partner questions process strategy

The player must respond.

These responses influence:

Risk
Client trust
Buyer interest

---

# 8. News & Market Feed

A secondary feed shows external developments.

These appear as **financial press headlines**.

Examples:

"Private Equity Fund Raises €2B Technology Fund"

"Strategic Buyer Announces Acquisition Program"

"Comparable SaaS Company Sold at 12x EBITDA"

These events influence the environment but do not require direct action.

They act as passive modifiers to the deal.

---

# 9. Event Notifications

Short notifications appear when something unexpected happens.

Examples:

Hidden workload triggered
Buyer expresses interest
Team fatigue event
Internal partner intervention

These notifications appear as quick banners.

---

# 10. Inbox Event Types

Emails can belong to different categories.

Client communication
Internal team messages
Buyer inquiries
Advisory input
Market intelligence

This allows filtering inside the inbox.

---

# 11. Narrative Feedback

After important actions the system generates narrative feedback.

Example:

"The Information Memorandum is shaping into a compelling narrative.
Several buyers are likely to show interest once the process launches."

These messages reinforce immersion.

---

# 12. News Headlines System

Headlines appear at random intervals.

They simulate:

market sentiment
industry developments
macro events

Example headlines:

"Tech valuations begin recovering after volatile quarter."

"Strategic consolidation accelerates in the payments sector."

These may subtly influence:

Buyer interest
Valuation potential
Deal momentum

---

# 13. Phase Transitions

At the end of each phase the interface shows a **Deal Progress Report**.

Example report:

Preparation Phase Complete

Buyer Universe: 34
Equity Story Strength: Strong
Data Quality: High

Projected Valuation Range: €120m–€150m

The player then moves to the next phase.

---

# 14. Interface Style

The visual tone should resemble a professional platform.

Colours:

Deep navy (finance / professionalism)
Muted gold accents (success / prestige)
Light neutral backgrounds

Typography should feel like a financial dashboard rather than a game HUD.

---

# 15. Player Identity

The player is positioned as:

"The M&A Rainmaker"

The interface should occasionally reference this role.

Examples:

"Your reputation as a rainmaker attracts additional buyers."

"Partners expect strong execution."

This reinforces the career fantasy of building a reputation in the M&A market.

---

# 16. Long-Term UI Goals

Future expansions may include:

Career mode
Deal reputation system
Buyer relationship memory
League tables
Advisory firm growth

These systems should integrate into the same workspace interface.
